# DJ Battle — The Video Game

> *Pokémon + Naruto Storm + Age of Empires + City Builder — set in the Low-Res Era of [[Planet Ongaku]]*

## Overview

**DJ Battle** is the first game in the DJ24 franchise, set during the [[Low-Res Era]] just before the [[High-Res Definition Era|HD Era]]. It combines open-world RPG elements, Pokémon-style top-down exploration, city-building mechanics, and Naruto Storm-style combat — all wrapped in an **African pixel art** aesthetic.

**Release Date:** September 27, 2023 (concept)

**Design Inspirations:**
| Influence | What It Contributes |
|---|---|
| Pokémon | Top-down exploration, rival mechanics, creature encounters |
| Age of Empires | City-building, resource management, strategic expansion |
| Naruto Storm / Mortal Kombat | Combat system with sound-based fighting |
| Cyberpunk / Edgerunners | World tone, tech aesthetic, narrative weight |
| GTA / Dragon Age | 3D city exploration after building placement |

## Story

### Act 1 — Introduction

The player (Pete/Garry/Ash equivalent) is introduced as a talented musician/DJ living in **Ongaku Prime**, where music rules everything. They are part of a prestigious music guild and have a strong reputation. They also have a **rival** — someone jealous of their skills.

### Act 2 — The Catalyst: Framed & Exiled

A massive event occurs — a sound system failure, corrupt frequency outbreak, or attack on the city's central music core. **The player is blamed.**

- The rival pins the blame on them
- The [[Harmony Council]] exiles them, stripping their title and status
- The player arrives in a **barren, empty wasteland** — the "Red Thingy Scene"

### Act 3 — Survival & City Building

Starting from scratch with basic tools, a simple beat machine, and a few supplies:
- Explore the area for resources
- Meet first NPC companion (neutral AI, rebel, or lost traveler)
- Basic city-building mechanics: shelter, sound energy, crafting music gear
- First structure: a recording booth or sound lab

### Act 4 — Recruiting People

| Recruitment Source | Description |
|---|---|
| **Wandering Exiles** | Others exiled for various reasons, seeking a new home |
| **Small Village Travelers** | NPCs from lesser-known settlements willing to relocate |
| **Disillusioned Musicians** | People in Ongaku Prime who've lost trust in the system |
| **Robots & AI Companions** | Sentient machines abandoned by [[NexaGen Harmonics]] |

Recruitment involves **mini-quests** (rescue missions, instrument retrieval, leadership tests) and **moral choices** (faction alignment, cultural decisions).

### Act 5 — Conflict with Ongaku Prime

1. **First Encounter:** Ongaku Prime sends scouts. *"You were supposed to disappear, not build something new."*
2. **Sneaking Back:** The player finds a way in, disguises themselves, can interact with old allies and markets
3. **The Truth:** The real culprits behind the framing are revealed. The rival works with [[NexaGen Harmonics]]. The player realizes they're leading a **rebellion**.

### Act 6 — City Expansion

The player's city becomes a real rival to Ongaku Prime:
- Music academies, sound labs, performance halls
- High-profile defectors and musicians join
- Territory gained through battle or diplomacy

### Act 7 — The Final Conflict

- The Harmony Council demands submission
- The rival makes their move — a major confrontation
- **Three possible endings:**
  1. Take back your place in Ongaku Prime
  2. Remain independent — build something new
  3. Destroy the corrupt system altogether

### The Girlfriend Twist

- The city also has a castle where the player's girlfriend has been taken
- When you finally reach the castle, **she attacks you** — you cannot use damage attacks
- She has been corrupted (bleached/darkened — evil form)

## Core Mechanics

### City Building (Hybrid 2D/3D)
The unique mechanic: build your city in a **top-down Age of Empires view**, then once buildings are placed, walk around in **3D** like GTA San Andreas or Dragon Age.

**Story triggers via building placement:**
- Mission 6: Meet Person X after placing the castle building → Choose between **Mage** or **Rogue Hybrid** first
- Cutscenes trigger outside newly-placed buildings
- Procedural mission design adapts to city layout

### Combat System

Each of the three [[Races and Classes|races]] fights differently:

#### 🟢 Humans (Harmonids) — Versatile Sound Masters
- **Style:** Structured, tactical, tempo-based
- **Signature Moves:**
  - *Beat-Sync Strikes* — rhythmic multi-hit combos
  - *Tempo Reversal* — shifts timing to disorient enemies
  - *Sonic Disruption* — mid-frequency perception distortion
  - *Turntable Blade* — circular sonic wave attack
  - *Acoustic Shield* — compressed sound defensive wall

#### 🦁 Animals (Sonari) — Instinct-Based Natural Warriors
- **Style:** Biological sound manipulation, species-specific advantages
- **Signature Moves:**
  - *Falcon Pulse* — high-frequency paralyzing shrieks (birds)
  - *Serpent Whisper* — low-frequency hypnosis (reptiles)
  - *Rhythm Dash* — untouchable synced movement (felines)
  - *Drumquake* — ground-shaking concussive force (beasts)
  - *Howling Echo* — pack amplification (wolves)

#### 🤖 Machines (Mechavibes) — AI-Driven Calculators
- **Style:** Pattern analysis, data-driven adaptation, synthetic waves
- **Signature Moves:**
  - *Glitch Step* — BPM-shifting teleportation
  - *Resonance Hacking* — corrupts enemy soundwaves
  - *Auto-Tune Armor* — frequency cancellation defense
  - *Overclock Surge* — near-instantaneous reaction speed
  - *Delay Feedback Loop* — creates confusing afterimages
