**"Deck of Cards"** is the fourth chapter of *[[Webcomic|DJ24: The War of Sound]]* and the preview finale of **Arc 1 — The First Drop**.

## Summary

[[Sync]] and [[Molly]] track the alert down into the rain-soaked lower districts of [[Ongaku Prime]]. In a neon alley, a giant holographic board unfolds in front of them — a fanned-out [[Deck of 52|deck of fifty-two cards]], each one showing the face of a different [[Sick 52]] soldier. Molly uses the moment to teach Sync how the enemy is organised. There are fifty-two of them, dealt out like a deck of cards, and the way you hunt them is from the bottom up. You start at the tens and you climb.

She explains the ranks. The Kings, Queens, and Jacks are the Sick 52's generals, and far above all of them sit the Aces. At the very top of the board, the Ace of Spades burns brightest of all — a silhouette none of them will even name. Sync asks the obvious question: why bother hunting the weakest cards first when it sounds backwards? Molly's answer is that the low cards act as bodyguards. Take them down, and the powerful cards lose the cover protecting them. You don't simply reach an Ace, she says — you earn it.

Their conversation ends when a low-card mutant bursts out of the rain-soaked dark, all jagged amplifiers and raw fury. Sync drops into **Bass Stance** to hold the narrow alley, and the mutant's charge smashes uselessly against his low-frequency barrier. Remembering his training — hold the ground, then take the head — Sync waits for the right moment and uses [[On-Beat Transition]] to switch stances on the beat, landing a bass-charged [[Bass Drop]] finisher. The card shatters into a burst of pixels.

On the holographic board, the defeated card flips face-down: one down, fifty-one to go. Molly admits it wasn't bad for someone who fights off the beat. But the victory is being watched. Far above the alley, on a rooftop in the rain, a hooded figure observes the entire kill. It is [[Red Silence]], and he almost smiles. He quietly tells the boy to keep climbing, because he will be waiting for him at the very top of the deck. This moment closes the opening preview of Arc 1.
