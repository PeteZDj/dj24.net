**"The Cursed Composer"** is the eighth chapter of *[[Webcomic|DJ24: The War of Sound]]*, continuing **Arc 1 — The First Drop**.

## Summary

The squad is called to the Grand Concert Hall in [[Ongaku Prime]], where a free show is taking place that nobody remembers signing up for. The audience sits unnaturally still, their eyes faintly glowing green. [[Molly]] realises with horror that the crowd has been trapped there for three days without food or water, doing nothing but listening.

On stage stands the culprit: [[Melody Hex]], the Queen of Clubs of the [[Sick 52]], known as the Cursed Composer. She raises her conductor's baton and the entire crowd rises as one, humming a single chord. She explains that every great composer needs an orchestra, and she has chosen to use the whole city as hers. Then she taunts [[Sync]] to strike her — because, she warns, her hostages feel every blow that lands on her.

When Sync lunges anyway, a hundred enthralled citizens step in front of her with their arms spread wide. Glowing green threads — her [[Puppet Chorus]] — run from her baton into every single person in the hall, turning the crowd into a living shield. Sync cannot land a hit without hurting an innocent.

Then he remembers [[Ninja Nagazaki]]'s lesson: you don't fight the sound, you cut the silence between the notes. Instead of attacking Melody Hex or the crowd, Sync drops onto the beat of her chord and waits for the tiny half-beat of silence between the notes. On that gap he snaps a tightly focused pulse — not at the people, but at the threads of music holding them. The [[On-Beat Transition]] timing lets him sever the strings cleanly.

The green threads shred and the freed citizens collapse, blinking awake, while Molly catches the falling people on a cushion of bass. Melody Hex is stunned that a child "off the beat" could cut her chorus, but she refuses to lower herself to fighting bodyguard's work. Cornered yet unbeaten, she dissolves into a swarm of green notes and escapes, promising they will meet again "at the crescendo." Molly grimly notes that a Queen is now loose in the city — which means the Sick 52's face cards are all beginning to move.
