**"Crescendo"** is the ninth chapter of *[[Webcomic|DJ24: The War of Sound]]*, continuing **Arc 1 — The First Drop**.

## Summary

The deck board at the Command Spire lights up, and the whole spade column is glowing. [[General 24]] connects the dots: the card left behind in Blue City was a spade, and the [[Sick 52]]'s sub-bass Jack hides behind his low cards — the ten, the nine, and the eight of his suit. Following the rule of the [[Deck of 52|deck]], the squad must clear those bodyguards before they can reach the face card at the top.

The path up is a tower of stacked speakers, miles high. [[Sync]], [[Molly]], and [[Ninja Nagazaki]] climb it together, with [[SubZ]]'s pirate crew covering the rear. Molly tells Sync to stay on tempo with her — they climb as one rhythm, or they fall as four.

Spade-suit mutants pour down the stairs to stop them, and the squad fights as a unit. Molly cuts through one rank with her [[Shockwave Pirouette]], Ninja erases another with his [[Silent Frequency]] blade, and Sync calls on SubZ to use [[Drop Theft]] — stealing the enemies' sound-energy and hurling it up the stairs to clear the way ahead. For the first time, Sync is fighting as part of a team rather than alone.

At the tenth landing they meet [[Nocturne Prime]], the 10 of Spades, cloaked in a deadening night-hum. He declares that no one climbs past the tenth step, because the Titan sleeps above and he is the alarm. Sync steps up to face him alone — calm now, feeling the steady beat running underneath all the chaos.

This is where his training pays off. Sync rides the rising tempo of the entire battle and times his stance-switch to its absolute peak. On the crescendo, he uses his [[On-Beat Transition]], snapping from bass to treble and blasting Nocturne Prime clean off the landing. With the bodyguards down, a vast door of black speaker-cones groans open above them, and a wave of crushing pressure rolls out — the exact same weight Sync felt back in MasterBass's temple. He knows instantly what it means: the sub-bass Jack is awake, and he is right behind that door.
