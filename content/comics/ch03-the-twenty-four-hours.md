**"The Twenty-Four Hours"** is the third chapter of *[[Webcomic|DJ24: The War of Sound]]*, continuing **Arc 1 — The First Drop**.

## Summary

At dawn over Ongaku Prime, a lattice of light hums quietly beneath the streets. This is the [[Frequency Grid]], and [[Molly]] explains to [[Sync]] just how important it is. Every wall, every light, and every heartbeat in the city runs on the Grid. If the sound is ever cut, the city itself dies. That, she tells him, is exactly what the [[Sick 52]] want: silence, forever. Understanding this is the reason DJ24 exists, Sync begins his training on the rooftop dojo at the top of the Command Spire.

Molly teaches him the two basic stances every DJ24 fighter must master. She flows into a spinning attack, her [[Shockwave Pirouette]], to demonstrate. There is **Bass Stance**, used to hold the ground and defend, and **Treble Stance**, used to strike fast and aim high. The rule is simple: pick one. When she presses the attack, Sync throws up a low-frequency barrier in Bass Stance, and her [[Prima Edge]] rings off the shockwave. But even as he blocks, Sync starts to think the rule is wrong — he shouldn't pick one stance, he should drop *between* them, on the beat.

He barely finishes the thought before a blade of near-invisible cyan light appears at his throat. [[Ninja Nagazaki]], DJ24's silent executioner, has crossed the entire rooftop without making a sound, his [[Silent Frequency]] blade already in place. He warns Sync that he makes far too much noise, and that noise is how the enemy finds you. Where Red Silence kills sound, Ninja explains, he himself kills the silence *between* sounds. Sync will need to learn both if he wants to survive.

Sync resets and tries again. This time he breathes, feels the city's pulse coming up through his feet, and waits. On the exact downbeat he uses [[On-Beat Transition]], switching from Bass Stance straight into Treble Stance so that the change itself becomes the attack. The combo detonates. Molly's next strike is parried clean, and Ninja's blade stops just short of his collar — a perfect draw against both of his teachers at once. Molly admits, with some surprise, that the boy can keep tempo after all.

The lesson ends the moment a red alert washes over the rooftop and a marker blooms on the city map far below. [[General 24]] announces that a [[Deck of 52|Sick 52 card]] has just surfaced in the lower districts — the lowest rank in the deck, and a perfect target for a first hunt. He orders Sync to bring it down and show him exactly what being "off the beat" can really do.
