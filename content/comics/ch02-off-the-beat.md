**"Off the Beat"** is the second chapter of *[[Webcomic|DJ24: The War of Sound]]*, continuing **Arc 1 — The First Drop**.

## Summary

The morning after the fight in the [[Echo Arena|Echo Arena]], [[Sync]] is escorted up through the DJ24 Command Spire in [[Ongaku Prime]] in a glass elevator, the whole city shrinking away beneath him. He is annoyed: he survived a death match against the most dangerous man alive, and DJ24 is treating him like a recruit who broke curfew.

The elevator opens onto a vast hall at the top of the spire. A golden, twenty-four-segment time-disc turns slowly above the room, and around it float the holographic portraits of twenty-four guardians — one for every hour of the day. Waiting beneath the disc is [[General 24]], the Supreme Commander of [[DJ24]]. He explains the structure of his organisation: twenty-four hours, twenty-four guardians. He does not fight like a normal soldier. Using [[The 24-Hour Schedule]], he positions his guardians so precisely that the enemy is always walking into the next planned event. As he tells Sync, he doesn't fight — he schedules reality.

General 24 then plays back a recording of the arena battle and points out the thing that troubles him. When Sync fought Red Silence, his timelines were flickering and out of step. He didn't play along to a beat that already existed — he locked onto one that *wasn't there*. There is no power like that anywhere on the General's charts, an early sign of the hidden ability that will later be called [[Phantom Alignment]]. He believes this is exactly why [[Red Silence]] chose to let Sync live.

Their conversation is interrupted when a blade-thin figure drops into the hall and lands lightly on the tips of her toes, twin silver edges retracting into her arms. This is [[Molly]], the Shockwave Prima, and the blades are her [[Prima Edge]]. She studies Sync with open doubt, unimpressed that this is the boy who supposedly frightened an unkillable man. Sync fires straight back that she doesn't look like much either for a ballerina carrying knives. After a tense beat, Molly almost smiles — he has passed her first small test.

General 24 makes his decision. He tells Sync that being "off the beat" makes him dangerous to the Sick 52 and dangerous to DJ24 alike, so he is going to keep the boy somewhere he can watch him closely. Sync is assigned to the elite **Prime Command Unit**. The General offers a hand wreathed in golden light; Sync hesitates, then takes it. For the first time, the clock of twenty-four hours has an extra, unscheduled guardian — Sync.
