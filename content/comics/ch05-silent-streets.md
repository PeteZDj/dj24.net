**"Silent Streets"** is the fifth chapter of *[[Webcomic|DJ24: The War of Sound]]*, continuing **Arc 1 — The First Drop**.

## Summary

A whole district has started to go quiet. In [[Blue City]], the rainy jazz quarter where saxophones usually echo from every club doorway, the sound is vanishing block by block. Neon signs flicker and die, music stops mid-song, and the people left inside the silence wander the streets in a daze, slowly forgetting that music ever existed at all. Because the threat is silence rather than force, [[General 24]] does not send a normal squad. He pairs [[Sync]] with [[Ninja Nagazaki]] — the one guardian who has spent his whole life learning to fight inside silence — and sends them in together.

At the edge of the dead zone, the two find the boundary between the living half of the street and the grey, soundless half. Sync's gauntlets begin to cut out the moment he crosses the line, just as they did against Red Silence, so he falls back on [[Resonance Sync]] to feel his way forward through the vibrations in the ground. Ninja moves differently. He treats the silence as his home, slipping ahead with his [[Silent Frequency]] blade drawn, reading the gaps where there should be noise and finding none.

They track the source to the centre of the district: a [[Deck of 52|Sick 52]] low card called the **Dead Air Revenant**. A hooded, flatlining figure, it feeds on the district's sound using a power called [[Dead Air]], spreading expanding bubbles of total silence and growing stronger with every block it drains. The Revenant is not as skilled as Red Silence, but its silence is colder and far wider, and it nearly overwhelms Sync. Every time he tries to make a sound, the bubble swallows it before it can land.

Ninja steps in with the lesson Sync needs. You do not fight the silence head-on, he tells him — you move inside it. Stop trying to shout over the dead zone and listen for the one rhythm still alive in it. Sync goes still and uses Resonance Sync again, and this time he feels it: the heartbeats of the trapped people, and a single, fading jazz note still hanging in the air. He locks onto that rhythm. On the exact beat he uses [[On-Beat Transition]] to switch stance and drives a [[Bass Drop]] straight into the Revenant, cracking the silence open. Ninja finishes the job, slipping in with [[Cutoff]] to strip the sound away from the creature itself and ending it with a single silent strike.

The instant the Revenant falls, the district's sound rushes back all at once — the clubs roar to life, the rain becomes loud again, and the dazed people blink as the music returns. But searching the spot where the creature died, Ninja finds a playing card left behind deliberately, the calling sign of a far higher rank in the [[Deck of 52|deck]]. The Dead Air Revenant was never just a stray monster; it was placed there on purpose, a probe sent to test how DJ24 reacts when the [[Frequency Grid]] is attacked from the edges. Back at the Command Spire, General 24 grimly notes that the [[Sick 52]] are no longer just raiding — they are measuring DJ24's defences, and the real climb up the deck is only beginning.
