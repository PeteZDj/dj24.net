# DJ24 — Ongaku's Private Super Army

<!-- IMAGE_PROMPT: "An elite squad of 24 armored DJ warriors standing in formation, each wearing futuristic headphones and wielding sound-based weapons, dramatic purple and blue lighting, sci-fi military anime style" -->

A decentralized elite unit serving as the planet's ultimate failsafe, funded by **Billionaire X** (father of [[General 24]]). They answer to no government.

- **24 Active Guardians** (one for each hour) and **54 Reserves**
- They are pattern recognizers, frequency controllers, and rhythm tacticians
- Their motto: *"Structure, Unity, Control."*

---

## Internal Factions

### The Harmonic Order
Led by [[General 24]]. Values structure, unity, and controlled, military-level discipline. They believe in containing the [[Sick 52]] through strategic superiority.

### The Mavericks
Led by [[Drez]]. Chaotic, rebellious, desiring to rule [[Planet Ongaku]] rather than just protect it. They clash constantly with the Harmonic Order.

### The Resonant Sons
Led by [[Sync]] and DJ24 Veterans. Believe music should be free, opposing authoritarian control from both the [[Harmony Council]] and their own leadership.

---

## Key Members

- **[[General 24]]** — Tactical, strict commander. Son of Billionaire X. Values precise order.
- **[[Sync]]** — The protagonist, a [[Syncopate]] with natural XDF abilities. Rebellious and questions the system.
- **[[Drez|Drez "The Unchained Beat"]]** — Leader of The Mavericks, chaotic and wildly aggressive.
- **[[Veyra|Veyra "Sonic Knight"]]** — The warrior who blends classical and electronic sound.
- **[[Nova|Nova "Frequency Queen"]]** — Dramatic performer manipulating synthwave laser attacks.

---

## Role in the Story

In **[[Season 1|Season 1: The Internal War]]**, DJ24 is Ongaku's primary defense against the [[Sick 52]]. The escalating conflict leads to the collapse of the [[Frequency Grid]].

By **[[Season 3|Season 3: The AI War]]**, [[General 24]] allies with AI musicians, fracturing DJ24 further. The unit's internal divisions mirror the larger conflict tearing Ongaku apart.
