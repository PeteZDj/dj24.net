# **Who Are the Sick 52?**

The **Sick 52** are **Ongaku’s most infamous underground faction**, a rogue movement of sound warriors, radicalized musicians, and sonic hackers who believe **music should never be controlled.** Once known as the **greatest soundmasters in history**, they were betrayed by the Harmony Council, exiled, and stripped of their power. Now, they are **outlaws**, waging an endless war against Ongaku’s rulers in an effort to **destroy the Frequency Grid and take back control of sound.**

### **🔥 What Do They Want?**

The Sick 52 **do not follow laws, rules, or structure.** Their ultimate goal is to **collapse the Harmony Council’s grip on music and free Ongaku from centralized control.** They believe that:✅ **The Frequency Grid is a prison** that limits creativity and suppresses natural sound.✅ **The Harmony Council is corrupt**, favoring certain genres and censoring others.✅ **The only way to fix Ongaku is to destroy its entire sound system and start over.**

### **🔥 How Do They Operate?**

Unlike DJ24, who use military tactics, the Sick 52 thrive on **chaos and unpredictability.**✅ **They do not have a single base**—they move between abandoned sound towers, underground studios, and hacked radio stations.✅ **They recruit from street musicians, club DJs, hackers, and disillusioned sound engineers.**✅ **They modify sound equipment into weapons**, using bass cannons, distortion rifles, and sonic mines.✅ **They sabotage Ongaku’s infrastructure**, hacking sound systems and cutting off music supply chains.

### **🔥 Their Ongoing War Against Ongaku**

For decades, the **Sick 52 have attempted to overthrow and take over Ongaku once every week.**❌ **They hijack government radio signals**, broadcasting unfiltered music in rebellion.❌ **They storm Harmony Council buildings**, attempting to take control of sound towers.❌ **They disrupt concerts, political speeches, and major DJ24 operations.**❌ **They take over entire districts**, turning them into lawless zones where only the underground sound thrives.

**But every time they take control, DJ24 or the Harmony Council fights back, pushing them out.**

This **never-ending war** has left Ongaku **divided**, with entire districts switching hands between the Council and the Sick 52 every week.

### **🔥 The Rise of Hip-Hop and the New Sick 52 Order**

Since the Frequency Grid collapse, **Hip-Hop has become the dominant genre of rebellion**, shaping the **next generation of Sick 52 fighters.** With DJ24 distracted by political battles and AI warfare, the Sick 52’s influence is **stronger than ever.**

-   **They control most underground clubs and street music hubs.**

-   **They manufacture and distribute Black Noise—a sound-based drug that alters perception.**

-   **They’ve turned entire city blocks into no-go zones for the Council.**

For the first time, **the Sick 52 are no longer just rebels—they’re rulers.** But the question remains:

🚀 **Will they free Ongaku… or destroy it completely?**

# 🧩 Important Design Advice

Do not treat all 52 equally.

You should structure them like:

-   5–8 main arc villains

-   10 recurring elites

-   20 regional bosses

-   14 special mechanic enemies

Otherwise it becomes bloated.

Naruto didn’t make every Akatsuki equal. Bleach didn’t give every Arrancar equal screen time.

Hierarchy = storytelling power.
