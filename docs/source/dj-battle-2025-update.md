Here’s a **basic story flow** that fits your vision and keeps things engaging for the player.

* * *

### **1\. Introduction – Setting the Stage**

The player (Pete/Garry/Ash equivalent) is introduced as a **talented musician/DJ** living in the main city of **Ongaku Prime**, where music rules everything. They are part of a **prestigious music guild** (or faction) and have a strong reputation.

They also have a **rival**, someone who is either jealous of their skills or sees them as an obstacle.

* * *

### **2\. The Catalyst – Framed & Exiled**

Something major happens—maybe a **massive sound system failure**, a **corrupt frequency outbreak**, or a **mysterious attack on the city's central music core**. The player is **blamed** for it.

-   Their **rival** or another authority figure **pins the blame** on them.

-   The **Harmony Council (the ruling body of Ongaku Prime)** exiles them, stripping them of their title and status.

-   The player is forced into the **Red Thingy Scene**—arriving in a barren, empty wasteland with nothing.

* * *

### **3\. Survival & First Steps in Building a City**

The player now has to **start from scratch**, with only basic tools, a simple beat machine, and a few supplies.

-   They explore the area, looking for resources.

-   They meet their **first NPC companion** (this could be a neutral AI, a rebel, or a lost traveler).

-   The game introduces **basic mechanics of city-building** (setting up a shelter, gathering sound energy, crafting basic music gear).

-   The player starts creating a **small settlement**—maybe with a single structure acting as a recording booth or sound lab.

* * *

### **4\. Recruiting People – The Need for a Community**

A city can't grow without people, so the player must go out and **find others** to join them.

**Ways they could recruit people:**

1.  **Wandering Exiles** – Others who were exiled for various reasons and are looking for a new home.

2.  **Travelers from Small Villages** – NPCs who live in smaller, lesser-known settlements and are willing to move if the player convinces them.

3.  **Disillusioned Musicians** – People in Ongaku Prime who **no longer trust the main city** and want to break away.

4.  **Robots & AI Companions** – Sentient machines looking for a purpose after being abandoned by NexaGen.

Recruitment can involve:

-   **Mini-quests** (helping them escape danger, retrieving an instrument, proving your leadership).

-   **Moral choices** (choosing between two factions or making key decisions about the town’s culture).

* * *

### **5\. Conflict with Ongaku Prime – The Player Can Return**

As the player grows their **own city**, they realize Ongaku Prime isn’t done with them.

1.  **First Encounter with Ongaku Prime Forces**

-   Ongaku Prime **sends scouts** to investigate.

-   They discover that the exiled player is **actually thriving**.

-   The scouts warn them: **"You were supposed to disappear, not build something new."**

3.  **The Player Gains Access Back to Ongaku Prime**

-   The player finds a **way to sneak back in**.

-   They **disguise themselves** to avoid detection.

-   They can now **interact with old allies, rivals, and markets**, but they must be careful.

5.  **Bigger Picture Unfolds**

-   The real **culprits behind the event** (that got them exiled) are revealed.

-   The **rival or antagonist’s plot deepens**—maybe they are working with NexaGen.

-   The player realizes they aren’t **just building a city**—they are **leading a rebellion**.

* * *

### **6\. Expansion – Growing the Player’s City**

At this point, the **player’s city grows into a real rival to Ongaku Prime**.

-   They build **infrastructure**: music academies, sound labs, performance halls.

-   They attract **more people, including high-profile musicians and defectors**.

-   Their influence starts to **spread**.

-   They gain **territory**, either peacefully or through clashes.

This opens up **different choices**:

-   Do they want to **fight for their return to Ongaku Prime**?

-   Or do they want to **overthrow the corrupted system** and establish a **new musical order**?

* * *

### **7\. The Final Conflict**

The player’s new city and Ongaku Prime **can no longer ignore each other**.

-   The **Harmony Council demands submission**.

-   The **rival makes their move**, leading to a **major confrontation**.

-   The final **battle** or **showdown** takes place.

-   The ending depends on the **player’s choices**:

-   **Take back their place in Ongaku Prime?**

-   **Remain independent and build something new?**

-   **Destroy the corrupt system altogether?**

* * *

### **Conclusion**

This flow ensures the player experiences:

1.  **Betrayal & Exile** → They lose everything.

2.  **Rebuilding** → They establish a new foundation.

3.  **Recruitment** → They gather allies and form a community.

4.  **Conflict & Espionage** → They deal with Ongaku Prime and uncover secrets.

5.  **Expansion** → Their city becomes a serious power.

6.  **Final Showdown** → They face off against the forces that exiled them.
