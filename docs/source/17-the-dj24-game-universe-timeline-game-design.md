## **1\. DJ Battle – The Low-Res Era (Pre-HD)**

**Genre:** **Tactical Rhythm Combat | Fighting Game | Dojo-Building | Tournament Progression**

### **Concept**

-   Set in the **Golden Age of Sound Combat**, before technology **disrupted the purity of music battles**.

-   Players travel across Ongaku, **challenging different dojos, mastering musical styles**, and competing in **historic DJ tournaments**.

-   No guns, no AI—just **pure sound combat**, with **genre-based fighting styles**.

### **Gameplay Mechanics**

-   **1v1 or Team-Based DJ Battles** (Like Pokémon but music-based).

-   **Tournament Progression** – Earn respect, unlock **new techniques, and challenge dojo masters**.

-   **City & Dojo Management** – As players win, they can **build and grow their own dojo**.

-   **Faction Rivalries** – Each faction represents a **different sound discipline (Hip-Hop, Rock, Jazz, etc.).**

### **How It Ends → Transition to DJ VIP**

-   **The LFO (Low-Frequency Oscillator) starts acting strangely**, disrupting music battles.

-   A **mysterious foreigner from Planet Scientifica arrives**, bringing **new technology** that starts shifting Ongaku into the **HD Era**.

-   This creates the divide between **natural music combat and technological enhancements**, setting up the **conflict for DJ VIP**.

* * *

## **2\. DJ VIP – The Early-HD Era**

**Genre:** **Narrative-Driven Social Sim | Performance-Based Career Progression | Corruption & Industry Politics**

### **Concept**

-   The first game where **music is no longer just about combat—it’s now an industry**.

-   The player is an **up-and-coming DJ/musician**, trying to **navigate fame, corruption, and the power shifts of the HD era**.

-   This game explores **how the world of music transitioned from purity to technology**.

### **Gameplay Mechanics**

-   **Performance & Reputation System** – Perform at clubs, **build a following, and choose between authenticity or selling out**.

-   **Business & Industry Politics** – Work with labels, **deal with corporate corruption**, or go independent.

-   **Early Cyber Enhancements** – Players **experiment with early tech upgrades**, leading into DJ24’s cyberpunk world.

-   **Underground vs. Commercial Music** – Will you support the **true art of music**, or join the **corporate-controlled industry**?

### **How It Ends → Transition to DJ24**

-   The **Harmony Council takes full control of the music industry**, implementing **the Single Beat System (SBS)** to regulate all music.

-   Some musicians **flee underground**, forming early **resistance movements**.

-   The first **generation of cyber-enhanced musicians** is born, leading into **DJ24**.

* * *

## **3\. DJ24 – The Start of the XD Era (10 Lifecycles After HD)**

**Genre:** **Open-World Cyberpunk RPG | City-Building | Real-Time Sound Combat**

### **Concept**

-   **Ongaku has entered the XD Era**, and music has **transcended planetary control**—sound can now **alter reality itself**.

-   The DJ24 are now **an elite, world-class private army**, protecting Ongaku’s sound-based power.

-   The **first interdimensional breaches appear**, leading to **new wars over sound manipulation**.

### **Gameplay Mechanics**

-   **Open-World Free Movement** (like Cyberpunk or GTA).

-   **City-Building & Political Influence** – Players control **parts of Ongaku, building defenses, industries, and managing music zones.**

-   **Real-Time Sound Combat** – High-speed, **rhythm-infused action fights**.

-   **Factions & Diplomacy** – Players must **balance power struggles** between the DJ24, NexaGen, and intergalactic forces.

### **How It Ends → Transition to DJ24: The Sick 52**

-   The first **Sick 52 uprising occurs** as NexaGen **experiments on sonic warfare soldiers**.

-   The DJ24 must decide: **Are they protectors, or are they just another oppressive force?**

* * *

## **4\. DJ24: The Sick 52 – Mid-XD Era**

**Genre:** **Squad-Based Tactical Sound Combat | City-Control Mechanics**

### **Concept**

-   After being **exiled and experimented on**, the **Sick 52 return to wage war on Ongaku.**

-   Players take on the role of **either a DJ24 commander or a Sick 52 rebel**, shaping the fate of the planet.

-   **This is a war game**—the battle for Ongaku’s survival.

### **Gameplay Mechanics**

-   **Squad-Based Sound Combat** – Players control a **team of elite warriors**, each with unique **musical abilities and tactical advantages**.

-   **City-Control Warfare** – The war unfolds in different districts, where **zones can be taken or lost**.

-   **Playable Dual Factions** – Players can either **defend Ongaku as DJ24** or **fight for revenge as the Sick 52**.

-   **Tactical Frequency Battles** – Combat is more **strategic**, similar to **XCOM or Total War**, but with a **music-powered twist**.

### **How It Ends → Transition to Planet Ongaku**

-   The war ends, but **the dimensional breaches worsen**.

-   The world **is now fully connected to intergalactic civilizations**, leading into **the next game**.

* * *

## **5\. Planet Ongaku – Post-XD Era / The Galactic Era**

**Genre:** **Space Exploration | Diplomacy | Music-Based Civilization Management**

### **Concept**

-   **Ongaku is no longer just a planet—it is now a universal power.**

-   Extra-Dimensional Frequencies (XDFs) **have permanently changed how civilizations function.**

-   The player takes on the role of **a leader, explorer, or warlord**, deciding **Ongaku’s place in the cosmos.**

### **Gameplay Mechanics**

-   **Space Exploration & Diplomacy** – Players can **travel between planets, form alliances, or conquer civilizations.**

-   **Universal Music Influence** – Some planets **embrace Ongaku’s sound-based power**, while others **fear it as a weapon.**

-   **Interstellar Warfare & Sound-Based Combat** – The ultimate evolution of **sound manipulation as a tool for intergalactic power.**

### **How It Ends → ???**

-   The game could end with **Ongaku becoming the ruling musical force of the universe… or fading into legend.**
