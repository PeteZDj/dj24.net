# 10. Reference What Other Stories Use

# **10\. Reference: What Other Stories Use?**

### **What Other Stories Use?**

Many great stories have a **"Destined One" or "Prophesied Hero"** trope, but they put their own spin on it:

-   **Dune (Paul Atreides)** → The **Kwisatz Haderach**, a prophesied messiah of the Fremen.

-   **Naruto (Naruto Uzumaki)** → The **Child of Prophecy**, destined to either save or destroy the ninja world.

-   **Star Wars (Anakin/Luke)** → The **Chosen One**, meant to bring balance to the Force.

-   **The Matrix (Neo)** → **The One**, the prophesied savior of humanity.

-   **Avatar: The Last Airbender (Aang/Korra)** → The **Avatar**, who reincarnates to maintain balance.

Now, we **create something new for Sync**—rooted in **music, rebellion, and a dystopian system.**

* * *

## **The Myth of the Syncopate**

### **Title: The Syncopate (or The Sync)**

**"When the rhythm is broken, a new beat must emerge."**

### **1\. The Legend**

-   Long ago, before Ongaku Prime ruled the world, music was **free and wild**, shaping reality itself.

-   The Harmony Council **took control**, enforcing a **Single Beat System (SBS)**—a rigid, controlled rhythm where only **approved music** is allowed.

-   However, legends speak of a **Syncopate**, a rare individual born **with the power to break the beat**—someone who **doesn’t follow the system but redefines it**.

* * *

### **2\. Who is Sync?**

-   Sync is **not just a name—it’s a title**. The last person who carried it was erased from history.

-   Sync was **never meant to exist**, yet they were born with the rare ability to **disrupt, remix, and reshape sound in ways no one else can.**

-   Their power is known as **Harmonic Disruption**—the ability to **break and reconstruct sound waves**, altering both music and reality itself.

* * *

### **3\. Why Was Sync Exiled?**

-   The **Harmony Council detected Sync’s power** at a young age.

-   They were either:

1.  **Born in Ongaku Prime** but their abilities were seen as a threat.

2.  **Discovered in an underground city**, where rebels hid children with unique music abilities.

-   The Council **orchestrated their exile**, labeling them as a criminal, believing that without training, Sync's power would fade away.

-   But instead of dying in exile, Sync **found the wasteland**, and the real story begins.

* * *

### **4\. The Rebellion & Sync’s Awakening**

-   Sync starts off **not believing in the legend**, thinking it’s just a **fairy tale**.

-   However, as their city grows and they **battle Ongaku Prime**, they start unlocking their true potential.

-   They begin to **hear echoes of past Syncopates**—whispers of musicians who defied the system before them.

-   The moment they fully awaken, **their sound breaks reality**—causing the **first true rhythm break in centuries**.

* * *

### **5\. The Meaning of Sync**

-   "Sync" **doesn’t just mean synchronization—it means breaking free from it.**

-   Sync’s final goal isn’t just **winning a war**—it’s **bringing back music’s true freedom** to the world.

-   But the question remains: **Are they truly the last Syncopate, or just the first of a new generation?**

* * *

### **Unique Terms That Could Be Used in the Game**

-   **Syncopate** → The one who breaks the rhythm.

-   **The Silence War** → A past rebellion where previous Syncopates were wiped out.

-   **The Single Beat System (SBS)** → The oppressive rhythm-control law of Ongaku Prime.

-   **Harmonic Disruption** → Sync’s power to break, remix, and reshape music.

-   **The Unheard** → Rebels who live in exile, waiting for the true sound to return.

* * *

### **Final Thoughts**

This gives **Sync a powerful lore**, similar to **The Chosen One trope but unique** to your game’s world. They aren’t just a savior—they are a **remixer of fate, a disruptor of control, and the first of something new**.
